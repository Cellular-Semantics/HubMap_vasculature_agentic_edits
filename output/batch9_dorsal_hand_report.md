## Anatomical Background

### Dorsal Metacarpal Arteries (UBERON:0002454)
The dorsal metacarpal arteries arise from the dorsal carpal arch, formed by anastomosis of dorsal carpal branches of the radial and ulnar arteries. The arch gives off three dorsal metacarpal arteries that run distally on the dorsal surface of the interosseous muscles between the metacarpal bones.

### Dorsal Digital Arteries of Manus (UBERON:0006146)
According to UBERON:0006146 definition: "Dorsal digital arteries arise from the bifurcation of dorsal metacarpal arteries. They travel along the sides and dorsal aspects of the phalanges of the middle finger, ring finger, and little finger. They communicate with the proper palmar digital arteries. They run with the dorsal digital nerves of ulnar nerve and dorsal digital nerves of radial nerve."

Each dorsal metacarpal artery typically bifurcates near the web spaces to form two dorsal digital arteries:
- **Lateral dorsal digital artery**: Supplies the lateral (radial) side of the digit
- **Medial dorsal digital artery**: Supplies the medial (ulnar) side of the digit
