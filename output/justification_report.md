## Row 81: interlobular artery of pancreas

**Reference:** N/A

### Populated Fields:


### Justification:

Interlobular arteries are intermediate-sized vessels in the pancreatic microvasculature that run between (inter) pancreatic lobules. Based on the anatomical hierarchy, they branch from larger pancreatic arteries such as the dorsal pancreatic artery (UBERON:8920020), greater pancreatic artery (UBERON:8920021), or caudal pancreatic artery (UBERON:8920022). They are located in the abdomen (UBERON:0000916) and supply the pancreas (UBERON:0001264). These arteries give rise to smaller branches including lobular arteries and periductal branches.

## Row 141: segmental hepatic artery

**Reference:** https://doi.org/10.1007/s12565-018-00475-x

### Populated Fields:

- **Definition:** An artery that branches from the sectorial hepatic arteries (left lateral, left medial, right anterior, or right posterior hepatic arteries) and supplies individual Couinaud segments of the liver. The segmental hepatic arteries represent the third-order branching of the hepatic arterial tree and provide blood supply to specific anatomical segments (I through VIII) of the liver.
- **Parent:** UBERON:0001637
- **Location:** UBERON:0002107
- **Connecting branch of:** UBERON:0015481|UBERON:0015482
- **Supplies:** UBERON:0001114

### Justification:

The paper by Garg et al. (2019) specifically focuses on evaluating the sectorial and segmental arterial branching (second- and third-order branching) in the right and left hemilivers. The abstract states that 'conventional segmental branching of both the right and the left hepatic arteries was seen in only 25% of the livers' and that the branching pattern was 'highly diverse'. Segmental hepatic arteries are crucial for procedures involving selective catheterization of individual liver segments.
